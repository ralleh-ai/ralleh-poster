# Poster Style Templates

Reusable style briefs to accelerate poster generation while maintaining artistic distinctiveness.

## Minimalist

**Aesthetic**: Negative space, typography-driven, reduced color palette (2-3 colors).

**Example Brief**: A single dominant typeface with large, clear letterforms. Simple geometric shape or subtle texture overlay. Maximum whitespace. Swiss Design influenced.

**Color Approach**: High contrast (e.g., deep navy + cream + single accent).

## Vintage / Retro Print

**Aesthetic**: Inspired by 1960s-1980s concert/event posters. Warm halftones, serif typography, slightly degraded texture.

**Example Brief**: Rich warm palette (oranges, mustards, deep reds). Hand-drawn appearance or retro photographic grain. Bold serif headlines.

**Color Approach**: Warm duotone or three-color risograph-like aesthetic.

## Bold Typography

**Aesthetic**: Letterforms dominate. Explosive, kinetic energy. Sans-serif weight variation.

**Example Brief**: Overlapping/stacked text creating layered depth. Dynamic angles, shadow/outline effects for readability. Energetic composition.

**Color Approach**: High contrast or neon accents against dark or light base.

## Abstract Conceptual

**Aesthetic**: Non-literal visual metaphors. Geometric patterns, fluid forms, or fragmented imagery.

**Example Brief**: Suggests event theme through abstract visual language rather than literal depiction.

**Color Approach**: Gradient, analog-color harmony, or bold accent against muted base.

**Note**: When drafting prompts, reference the specific style name and reference the DESIGN_STANDARDS to avoid slop.
